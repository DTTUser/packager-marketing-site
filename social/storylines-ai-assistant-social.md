## LinkedIn (primary channel)

Opener has to work before the "see more" fold. Link goes in the first comment.

Articulate says Storyline's new AI Assistant keeps you "in the driver's seat." Look at what it is actually driving, and it gets a lot more interesting.

I went through what shipped in Storyline 360's AI Assistant across the last two quarterly releases. It generates voiceover from AI voices. It syncs timelines to your narration automatically. It builds a colour palette from a reference image. It polishes copy for tone and grammar.

All genuinely useful. All of it is content, media, or formatting.

Here is what is not in any release note this year. It does not write a variable. It does not build a branching path. It does not wire up a trigger. Ask it for a knowledge check that sends learners down different paths based on their answer, and you are doing it exactly the way you did in 2019.

That is not a flaw. It is a choice. Automating a colour palette is low risk. Automating branching and variable logic is the part of the job people actually pay a licence for, so of course the AI stops exactly where that value begins.

"You stay in charge" is true. But in charge of the easy 80 percent, because the AI took it off your plate. The hard 20 percent is still entirely yours, because nobody has offered to touch it.

There is a different version of "in charge," where the AI does the hard part too and you are supervising the whole thing, not just the bits that were safe to automate. Wrote up the full comparison. Link in the comments.

**Title:** Storyline's AI Assistant says you stay "in charge." In charge of what, exactly?

**Body:**

Articulate added an AI Assistant to Storyline 360 this year, and the marketing line is that you stay "in the driver's seat," it "speeds up course creation without taking over." I went and checked what it actually does across the two feature releases so far this year.

It generates AI voiceover. It auto-syncs timelines to narration. It builds a colour palette from a reference image. It polishes text for tone and grammar. Genuinely useful stuff, and I would use all of it if I were still building in Storyline.

What it does not do, in any release note this year: write a variable, build a branching path, wire up a trigger, or construct a scored interaction. Ask it for a knowledge check that branches based on the answer and you are doing it by hand, same as always.

I do not think that is a weakness in the feature. I think it is deliberate. Automating a colour palette is low risk. Automating the branching logic is automating the actual reason a lot of teams still pay per seat instead of hiring a developer, so of course the AI does not go near it.

Disclosure since it is relevant: I build a SCORM packaging tool for AI-generated HTML, so I have a stake in this argument. Wrote the longer version here if you want it: https://packager.dtttech.com/blog/storylines-ai-assistant-and-the-part-it-wont-touch.html

Genuinely curious whether anyone here has tried briefing Storyline's AI Assistant for anything beyond copy, voice, or colour and gotten further than I think it goes. Would like to be wrong about this.
