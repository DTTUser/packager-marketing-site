What it actually does

SCORM is a standard that lets a learning management system track who completed your content, what they scored, and how long they spent. Most authoring tools force you into their ecosystem to get there. This does it from any HTML.

  • ✓

    Wraps your HTML in a SCORM 1.2 manifest

    Adds the imsmanifest.xml file, the SCORM API wrapper script, and the supporting files an LMS expects. Your original HTML stays exactly as it was.

  • ✓

    Tracks completion in any compliant LMS

    Verified end to end on Talent LMS. Should work in any SCORM 1.2 LMS, which is most of them, including Moodle, Cornerstone, Docebo, and SCORM Cloud.

  • ✓

    Per-exercise tracking if your HTML supports it

    If your content calls the SCORM interactions API, the LMS will report per-question results. If it only reports completion at the end, that is what gets tracked.

  • ✓

    Browser-only, no install

    Runs in your browser. Your content never leaves your machine. The output zip is yours, with no DRM, no watermark, no platform lock-in.
